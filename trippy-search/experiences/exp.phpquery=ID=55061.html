Fundamental #7: When using a strong psychoactive...take oneself "off duty" from responsibilities that might be interfered with (job, child care, etc.), and arrange for someone else to be "on duty".
Weaving a Story, Drawing a Graph
Nitrous Oxide
by Z-Person

 
DOSE:   repeated inhaled Nitrous Oxide (gas)

BODY WEIGHT: 140 lb


I was a bit bored so I decided to try a charger of nitrous, it was the most amazing nitrous experience I’ve ever had. I charged up my cream whipper and hyperventilated for a few seconds, then inhaled deeply. I hit me instantly. I could hear Startrek esque sounds as I was going deeper and deeper under, a sound like a ricochet, or a space ship. But with gears, on the way up it went up a gear, and as I came down it went down a gear. When I was completely sober the sound of cars going past the window was morphed in such a way it sounded exactly like that sound. Also I saw a green/yellow pixilate when I came out of the nitrous induced state.

Due to that last experiment into solitary nitrous oxide use, I decided to try using 2 chargers at once. I must’ve been completely knocked out, I had utterly amazing hallucinations. Unfortunately I can’t remember the bulk of them now. But I woke with the same sound, and it began with the same sound. Also the whipper was left on my face with still some gas left, I really do think I was unconscious. The hallucinations had something about the desert, and when I was waking up I remember a family waving good-bye to me. It’s like I was shot into oblivion and then pulled back again. Oh and of course this is all very comfortable, innocent and euphoric.

I go on to repeat this a few more times, the hallucinations get even better. I’m noticing the reverse tolerance. I have a pattern, which seems to correspond to all the trips. Every time it’s stronger. In the trip on the 8th charger the graph of the trip got so unbelievably beautiful. 9th even better! I’m making sure that the 10th is my last however. Each time it gets exponentially more detailed. It’s absolutely amazing, and I really want to do an 11th, but I mustn’t. Or I’ll soon finally find myself using a lot… daily! It’s the most psychologically addictive thing since the snooze alarm. It fills me with a complete sense of utter urgency requiring me to take more nitrous, just to hear the next part of the story.

The nitrous tells a story in it’s trip. It shows me a pattern, a graph of infinitely intricate detail. Each and every detail in the graph corresponds and seems to pre-empt things that I hear or see or do. I.e. if the song I’m listening to has a symbol crash, BEFORE I hear the crash the graph spikes.
